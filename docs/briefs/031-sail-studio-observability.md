# Slice Brief 031 - SAIL Studio Observability and Visualization

**Date:** 2026-07-22

## Objective

Complete P1-16 by extending the existing read-only, phone-friendly Studio with
one receipt-bound SAIL investigation surface. A reviewer must be able to move
from ranked episodes through synchronized residuals, missingness, belief
revision, structural surprise, acquisition, posterior/invariance, and
TwinWorthiness without crossing proof classes or opening a detached viewer.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The default ranking retains the strongest interpretable partial episodes and
  may omit only explicitly classified no-signal runs.
- Replay synchronizes action, measured and simulated joints, end effector,
  pawn, target, aperture, timing, contact, consequence, phase, and available
  licensed frames; every absent channel is visibly marked missing.
- Residual heatmaps/confidence overlays, belief graph before/after views,
  compensation debt, intervention signatures, posterior/invariance summaries,
  and the current failed/not-evaluable TwinWorthiness gates bind the evaluator
  receipts that produced them.
- Paper figures export from those same bound artifacts rather than a parallel
  data path.
- Video and 3DGS remain visual-only unless separately admitted, simulation,
  learned-policy, physical read-only, and physical-task labels remain distinct,
  and no visual creates training, promotion, or physical authority.
- The existing Studio remains read-only and usable at mobile viewport widths;
  deterministic asset compilation and browser/UI tests pass.

## Stop Conditions

- A new detached visualization app replaces or forks the existing Studio.
- Missing data are rendered as zeros, confidence, or measured channels.
- A visual uses a different artifact or receipt than its evaluator source.
- Weak/no-signal runs displace the strongest partial evidence without an
  explicit ranking reason.
- Video, 3DGS, simulation, policy, or physical evidence is relabeled into a
  stronger proof class.
- Any write, training, policy selection, hardware, or physical authority is
  exposed from the Studio.
