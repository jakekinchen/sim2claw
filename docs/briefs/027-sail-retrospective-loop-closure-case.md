# Slice Brief 027 - SAIL Retired-Workcell Loop-Closure Case

**Date:** 2026-07-22

## Objective

Complete P1-12 by reconstructing the retired-workcell calibration history
through the fixed-pad, timestep, friction, and retained RMS closeouts; compare
chronological, shuffled, no-revisit, full-batch, and frozen SAIL
reconstructions; and issue the current evaluator-owned TwinWorthiness
certificate without relabeling retrospective evidence as fresh validation.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Every graph edit binds to an existing intervention/result and preserves the
  byte-identical source action descriptor.
- Boundary fits, reversals, mixed-vector changes, coverage without one-model
  success, and explanatory-versus-measured-parameter distinctions are explicit.
- Influence discovery and sparse loop closure use the frozen P1-07 method.
- Chronological, seeded shuffled-order, no-loop-closure, full-batch, and SAIL
  results are compared with exact receipts and paper-ready figures.
- The certificate keeps TW-G2 and TW-DATA closed unless the frozen evidence
  genuinely satisfies their evaluator-owned requirements.

## Stop Conditions

- Any retrospective row is relabeled prospective, fresh held-out, or measured
  physical parameter evidence.
- A graph edit lacks a source intervention/result binding.
- Action bytes, evaluator state, or certificate authority changes.
- The case attempts new physical capture, robot motion, training, or policy
  selection.
